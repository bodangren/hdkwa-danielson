# Product Guidelines

## Voice and Tone
- **Instructional & Action-Oriented:** Focus on "how-to" and immediate application.
- **Encouraging & Professional:** Acknowledge the challenges of teaching while providing supportive, high-signal solutions.
- **Authoritative yet Accessible:** Leverage the Danielson Framework and expert literature with clear, jargon-free language.

## User Experience (UX) Principles
- **Efficiency First:** Teachers are busy. Information must be scannable and videos must be easily accessible.
- **Visual Progress:** Use clear visual cues (checkmarks, progress bars, badges) to motivate and track implementation.
- **Mobile Friendly:** Ensure the site is usable on tablets and phones for "on-the-go" reference in the classroom.
- **Single Source of Truth:** Clearly link every strategy back to the Danielson Framework domains to ensure relevance to observations.

## Design Aesthetic
- **Clean and Academic:** Use a professional palette that feels educational but modern.
- **Instructional Clarity:** Use consistent layouts for "Actionable Modules" (Title, Step-by-Step, Video, Source).
- **Interactive Feedback:** Subtle animations and feedback for progress tracking (e.g., ticking a box).

## Content Guidelines
- **Video Integration:** Use responsive embeds. Provide a brief "Why this video?" caption for context.
- **Source Citations:** Always credit the primary text (e.g., "Strategy 12 from TLAC 3.0").
- **Danielson Alignment:** Explicitly state which component of the framework (e.g., 2b: Establishing a Culture for Learning) each strategy supports.
